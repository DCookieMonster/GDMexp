“The traveling salesman“ game is a single player game.

Each vertex on the graph is analogous to a City and the edges are analogous to roads between the cities.

The purpose of the game is to choose a route that visits all cities without visiting any of the cities more than once.

Opening Page

Your route can start from any city in the graph.

You can mark the starting point of the route - by clicking one of the cities.

Possible Actions

You can choose a city by clicking on a vertex. Once you select a city, edges (roads) leading to your next possible destinations (cities) will be colored green.

The blue arrows mark the route you have taken so far.

You can move forward in completing your route by selecting one of the cities that are connected to green edges (roads).

In the example screenshot, it can be seen that the route started at city 1 and the last action was choosing city 5. The next possible destination cities are 2,3 or 4.

At any stage, you can backtrack as many steps as you wish by clicking on the last selected city, or by clicking the "UNDO MOVE“ button.

Once you have completed a route that you think is correct, submit it by clicking the "Continue" button. Once you’ve submitted your solution you will not be able to change it.

The Task

Your task is to choose a route that connects all cities without passing through the same city more than once.

An example correct solution is shown in the screenshot

Note

If you have already visited all cities that are connected to the selected city, green pathways will not appear since additional destinations are no longer accessible (as you cannot visit a city more than once).
